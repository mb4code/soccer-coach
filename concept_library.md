# U13 Soccer Team Dynamics App Plan

## Team Shape

The main lineup is:

```text
        LF        CF        RF

        LM        CM        RM

        LD        CD        RD

                 SW

                 GK
```

Where:

- **LF / CF / RF** = three forwards
- **LM / CM / RM** = three midfielders
- **LD / CD / RD** = three defenders
- **SW** = sweeper
- **GK** = goalie

The app should focus on teaching full-field team movement, not individual dribbling or ball-handling skills.

The simplified teaching model is built around two big ideas:

1. **Creating Space**
2. **Moving Together**

---

# Big Idea 1: Creating Space

Creating space means helping the team make the field bigger, giving the player on the ball better options, and creating chances to score.

This section includes four concepts:

1. Spacing: width and depth
2. Support angles around the ball
3. Through balls and timed runs
4. Crosses with organized box runs

---

## 1. Spacing: Width and Depth

### Key Concept

When the team has the ball, the goal is to make the field **wide and deep**.

This does not mean players should simply stand far apart. It means players should spread out in useful positions that create passing options and open space.

In this formation:

- The **left forward** and **right forward** stretch the field wide.
- The **center forward** stays high to stretch the defense vertically.
- The **midfielders** create passing options underneath the forwards.
- The **defenders and sweeper** provide safe options behind the ball.

### What Players Should Learn

Good spacing gives the player on the ball:

- Room to pass or carry the ball forward
- A forward option
- A sideways option
- A backward safety option
- More space between defenders

Bad spacing makes the field crowded and easier to defend.

### App Demonstration

The app can show a possession moment and ask:

> Is the team making the field big?

Possible interactions:

- Show players too close to the ball.
- Show the same situation with better width and depth.
- Let the user move players into better spacing.
- Score the shape based on whether:
  - Both sides of the field are occupied
  - A forward is stretching the defense
  - There is a safe pass backward
  - Players are too close together
  - The ball carrier has useful options

### Simple Player Phrase

> Make the field big when we have it.

---

## 2. Support Angles Around the Ball

### Key Concept

The player with the ball should not be isolated.

Nearby teammates should create **passing angles**, not straight lines.

A useful support shape gives the ball carrier:

1. A forward option
2. A sideways or inside option
3. A backward safety option

### Example

If the **right defender** has the ball, support could come from:

- The **right midfielder** ahead
- The **center defender** inside
- The **sweeper** behind
- The **right forward** higher up the line

The important idea is that teammates move so they are visible and passable.

### What Players Should Learn

A teammate is not helping just because they are nearby.

They are helping if:

- The ball carrier can see them
- A defender is not blocking the passing lane
- They are at an angle
- They give the ball carrier a useful choice

### App Demonstration

The app can freeze play with one player on the ball and ask:

> Who is supporting the ball?

Possible interactions:

- Highlight good support options.
- Show when a teammate is hidden behind a defender.
- Let the user move supporting players into open passing lanes.
- Rate the support shape based on whether players are:
  - At a good angle
  - Too flat
  - Too far away
  - Hidden behind an opponent
  - Providing a safe option

### Simple Player Phrase

> Give the ball an angle.

---

## 3. Through Balls and Timed Runs

### Key Concept

A through ball is not just a forward pass.

It requires two actions working together:

1. A passer who sees space behind the defense
2. A runner who times the run into that space

The pass should usually go **into space**, not directly to the player’s feet.

### What Players Should Learn

Forwards should not run behind too early.

They need to learn to:

- Stay onside
- Wait until the passer has the ball under control
- Start the run when the passer can see them
- Run into the space behind or between defenders
- Curve the run when needed to stay onside

Midfielders should learn to:

- Look up early
- Recognize when a defender steps forward
- Pass into the space behind the line
- Time the pass with the runner’s movement

### Common Through-Ball Combinations

In this formation, common through-ball patterns include:

- **CM to CF**
- **LM to LF**
- **RM to RF**
- **CM to LF or RF on a diagonal run**
- **LF or RF inside to CF**
- **CM into space between an outside defender and center defender**

### App Demonstration

The app can create a scenario where a midfielder has the ball and the forwards are facing a defensive line.

The app can ask:

> When should the forward run?

Possible demonstrations:

- Run too early: offside or easy to mark
- Run too late: defender recovers
- Run at the right time: through ball creates a scoring chance

Possible interactions:

- Let the user choose when to trigger the forward’s run.
- Let the user choose whether the pass goes to feet or into space.
- Score the action based on:
  - Timing
  - Onside position
  - Space behind the defense
  - Pass direction
  - Defensive pressure on the passer

### Simple Player Phrase

> Pass into space, run onto it.

Another useful phrase:

> Run when the passer can see you.

---

## 4. Crosses with Organized Box Runs

### Key Concept

A cross is a team action, not just one player kicking the ball into the box.

When the ball goes wide, the forwards and midfielders need to attack different areas.

The team should not all run to the same spot.

### Main Crossing Runs

For U13 players, keep the box runs simple:

1. **Near-post run**
2. **Middle run**
3. **Far-post run**
4. **Cutback option**
5. **Top-of-box option**

### Role Examples

In this formation:

- The **center forward** often attacks the middle or near post.
- The **opposite-side forward** attacks the far post.
- The **ball-side forward** may cross, combine, or make a near-post run.
- The **center midfielder** or opposite midfielder can arrive at the top of the box.
- The **ball-side midfielder** supports behind the wide player.
- The **defenders and sweeper** stay ready behind the attack.

### What Players Should Learn

When the ball goes wide:

- Do not all run straight to the goal.
- Do not all stand and watch.
- Attack different spaces.
- Arrive as the cross is played, not too early.
- Keep one player available for a cutback.
- Keep some players behind the ball for safety.

### App Demonstration

The app can place the ball wide with the left forward or right forward and ask:

> Where should the runners go?

Possible demonstrations:

- Bad version: all three forwards run to the same spot.
- Better version:
  - One player attacks the near post.
  - One player attacks the middle.
  - One player attacks the far post.
  - One midfielder stays available for a cutback or top-of-box shot.
  - One support player stays behind the ball.

Possible interactions:

- Let the user assign runs:
  - Near post
  - Far post
  - Penalty spot
  - Top of box
  - Stay back for safety

The app can evaluate:

- Are the box runs spread out?
- Is the far post covered?
- Is there a cutback option?
- Is there protection behind the attack?

### Simple Player Phrase

> Attack different spaces in the box.

# Big Idea 2: Moving Together

Moving together means the team shifts as one connected group instead of acting like separate players.

This section includes two concepts:

1. Team shape moves with the ball
2. Back line steps up when attacking

---

## 5. Team Shape Moves with the Ball

### Key Concept

The team should move as one connected group.

- When the ball moves left, the team shifts left.
- When the ball moves right, the team shifts right.
- When the ball moves forward, the team moves forward.
- When the ball goes backward, the team adjusts and supports.

The goal is to avoid having ten field players making ten separate decisions.

### What Players Should Learn

When attacking:

- Players near the ball support the ball carrier.
- Far-side players stay connected instead of standing completely isolated.
- The team keeps width, but not so much distance that players cannot help.
- The midfield connects the forwards to the defenders.
- The sweeper and goalie stay connected behind the team.

When defending:

- Ball-side players apply pressure.
- The middle stays protected.
- Far-side players tuck in.
- The sweeper reads danger behind the defensive line.

### Example: Ball on the Left Side

Expected team behavior:

- **LF** gives width or attacks forward space.
- **LM** supports underneath.
- **CM** shifts left-center.
- **RM** tucks inside.
- **RF** stays available but does not become disconnected.
- **LD** supports behind.
- **CD and RD** shift across.
- **SW** stays central behind the defensive line.

### Example: Ball in the Middle

Expected team behavior:

- Both sides stay available.
- Forwards stretch the defense.
- Midfielders create support angles.
- Defenders stay connected.
- Sweeper provides security behind the defensive line.

### Example: Ball on the Right Side

The team shape mirrors the left-side behavior:

- **RF** gives width or attacks forward space.
- **RM** supports underneath.
- **CM** shifts right-center.
- **LM** tucks inside.
- **LF** stays available but connected.
- **RD** supports behind.
- **CD and LD** shift across.
- **SW** stays central behind the defensive line.

### App Demonstration

The app can move the ball to different field areas and ask:

> How should the team move?

Possible ball locations:

- Left defense
- Center defense
- Right defense
- Left midfield
- Center midfield
- Right midfield
- Left forward
- Center forward
- Right forward

The app can measure:

- Is the team connected?
- Are players too spread out?
- Are players too bunched?
- Is the far side tucked in enough?
- Is there support around the ball?
- Is the sweeper connected behind the line?

### Simple Player Phrase

> The ball moves, we move.

---

## 6. Back Line Steps Up When Attacking

### Key Concept

When the team attacks, the defenders and sweeper cannot stay too deep.

They need to move up so the team stays connected.

This is especially important in this formation because the team has:

- Three defenders
- One sweeper
- One goalie

The sweeper provides extra protection, but the sweeper should not become a reason for the defenders to stop moving with the team.

### What Players Should Learn

When the forwards and midfielders attack, the defensive group should come with them.

That means:

- The **three defenders** move toward midfield.
- The **sweeper** also moves up, staying behind the defenders.
- The **goalie** moves higher as the team moves higher.
- The team stays compact enough to win the ball back if it is lost.

### Why This Matters

If the back line stays too deep:

- The midfield gets isolated.
- There is a huge gap in the middle.
- Loose balls are harder to win.
- The opponent has space to counterattack.
- The forwards are disconnected from the rest of the team.

If the back line steps up properly:

- The team can keep pressure on the opponent.
- The midfield has support.
- The sweeper can clean up balls behind the defenders.
- The opponent has less room to counter.
- The team can attack again quickly after a clearance.

### Important Safety Rule

The back line should step up when there is **pressure on the ball**.

They should be more careful when the opponent has time to look up and play a long ball.

Teach the players:

```text
Pressure on the ball = step up
No pressure on the ball = be ready to drop
```

### App Demonstration

The app can show an attack near the opponent’s goal and ask:

> Where should the defenders and sweeper be?

Possible demonstrations:

#### Bad Version

- Forwards are attacking.
- Midfielders are near the play.
- Defenders are still near their own box.
- Sweeper is too deep.
- There is a huge gap between midfield and defense.

App message:

> The team is stretched. If we lose the ball, the opponent has too much space.

#### Good Version

- Defenders step near midfield.
- Sweeper stays behind them as protection.
- Goalie also moves up slightly.
- Team is connected from front to back.

App message:

> The team is connected. We are ready to attack again or defend quickly.

Possible interaction:

- Let the user move the defensive group up or back.

The app can score:

- Gap between midfield and defense
- Sweeper’s distance behind defenders
- Goalie’s connection to sweeper
- Whether there is pressure on the ball
- Risk of a ball over the top

### Simple Player Phrase

> When we attack, our defense comes with us.

Another useful phrase:

> Step up when there is pressure. Drop when there is not.

# Suggested App Structure

The app can be organized into two main sections and six lessons.

---

## Section 1: Creating Space

### Lesson 1: Make the Field Big

**Concept:** Spacing: width and depth  
**Player takeaway:** Use the whole field.  
**App demo:** Ball is with one player. The user adjusts teammates. The app checks width, depth, and useful passing options.

### Lesson 2: Help the Ball

**Concept:** Support angles around the ball  
**Player takeaway:** Give the ball an angle.  
**App demo:** Ball carrier is selected. The user moves nearby teammates. The app checks whether support players are actually open.

### Lesson 3: Run Behind at the Right Time

**Concept:** Through balls and timed runs  
**Player takeaway:** Pass into space, run onto it.  
**App demo:** A midfielder has the ball. A forward waits, then runs. The user controls timing. The app shows early, late, and correct runs.

### Lesson 4: Attack the Box Together

**Concept:** Crosses with organized box runs  
**Player takeaway:** Attack different spaces.  
**App demo:** Ball is wide. The user assigns runs into the box. The app checks near post, middle, far post, cutback, and safety.

---

## Section 2: Moving Together

### Lesson 5: Shift with the Ball

**Concept:** Team shape moves with the ball  
**Player takeaway:** The ball moves, we move.  
**App demo:** Ball moves around the field. The user adjusts team shape. The app checks whether players are connected, shifted, and balanced.

### Lesson 6: Bring the Back Line Up

**Concept:** Back line steps up when attacking  
**Player takeaway:** When we attack, the defense comes with us.  
**App demo:** Team attacks in the opponent’s half. The user positions defenders, sweeper, and goalie. The app checks whether the team is compact or stretched.

---

# Final Simplified Teaching Model

## Creating Space

1. **Make the field big.**
2. **Give the ball an angle.**
3. **Run into space at the right time.**
4. **Attack different spaces in the box.**

## Moving Together

5. **The ball moves, we move.**
6. **When we attack, the defense comes with us.**

This gives the app a simple foundation: two big ideas, six lessons, and clear player language that connects directly to full-field 11v11 team play.
